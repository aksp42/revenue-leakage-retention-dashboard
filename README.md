#  Revenue Leakage & Customer Retention Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes the Olist E-Commerce dataset to identify hidden revenue loss caused by low customer retention.

Many businesses focus heavily on acquiring new customers, but sustainable growth comes from retaining existing ones. This dashboard highlights where revenue is leaking and what actions businesses can take to improve Customer Lifetime Value (CLV).

The analysis was performed using Power BI, transforming raw e-commerce data into actionable business insights.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem
Is the business actually growing, or just acquiring new customers without retaining them?

The analysis reveals a "Leaky Bucket Problem" — customers purchase once but rarely return, causing significant revenue loss.

## 📊 Dashboard Structure

### 1️⃣ Executive Health Check (KPIs)

This section provides a quick overview of business health through key metrics.

**Key KPIs**
- Total Revenue vs Target
- Customer Retention Rate
- Average Order Value (AOV)

These indicators help identify whether the business is growing sustainably or losing returning customers.

---

### 2️⃣ Revenue Leakage Analysis (Cohort Analysis)

**Visual Used:** Heatmap / Cohort Matrix

This section analyzes customer retention over time.

It tracks customers acquired in a specific month and shows how many of them return in the following months.

**Insight**
If the retention percentage declines every month, it signals a major revenue leakage issue.

---

### 3️⃣ Customer Churn Drivers

**Visual Used:** Bar Chart (Review Score vs Product Category)

This section investigates why customers stop purchasing.

**Key Insight**
Product categories with lower review ratings show higher churn patterns.

When customer satisfaction decreases, repeat purchases also decline.

---

## 🔍 Key Insights from the Analysis

### The Retention Gap
The business relies heavily on acquiring new customers.

- 98% New Customers
- 2% Returning Customers

This imbalance results in an estimated **$1.99M potential revenue gap**.

---

### The Quality–Churn Connection
Product categories with lower customer ratings experience higher churn rates.

Poor product quality directly impacts:
- Customer trust
- Repeat purchases
- Brand loyalty

---

### The Strategic Shift
Growth is not only about acquiring customers.

Businesses that focus on:
- Customer experience
- Product quality
- Retention strategies

can significantly improve **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)**.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendation

Based on the analysis, one possible action is:

If the Electronics category has long delivery times (more than 10 days), customers are less likely to return.

Reducing delivery time by 3 days in this category could increase repeat purchases and potentially improve sales by **5%**.
